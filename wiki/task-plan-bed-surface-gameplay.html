Bed Surface Gameplay

Pilot Proven 2026-06-02

Plan to make the bed feel like a real raised surface: route kitties through an approach point, mount onto the mattress, dismount back to the floor, and only split bed art if the no-cut render pass fails.

Mental Model

Y-sort works beautifully for entities that stand on the same floor plane. Kitties interacting with each other look natural because each kitty's node origin behaves like a feet position. Furniture surfaces are different: a bed has height, and the kitty is sometimes on the floor near it and sometimes on top of it.

Floor object

One origin and one visual layer are usually enough. Sort by Y. Lower on screen draws in front.

Surface object

The object needs at least two character render states. Floor kitties can read as under/behind the bed; mounted kitties can read as on top of it.

Approach point

The character walks to a reachable floor point near the object. This is what navigation and obstacle avoidance understand.

Occupy point

The character transitions onto the object and holds a pose at the surface hotspot. Leaving the bed should transition back through the approach point before normal pathing resumes.

For the current bed art, the visible occupancy spots do not appear to sit behind or under a separate bed part. That suggests the first pilot should keep one bed sprite: floor kitties can pass under/behind it, while hotspot occupants render above the whole bed.

Standard 2D pattern: path to the interactable edge, play a small mount/snap transition, render the character in an object-specific mounted state, then dismount back to the edge before leaving. A single global y-sort should not carry all of that meaning.
Navigation caveat: the current NavigationRegion2D covers floor space inside the walls, but observed kitty behavior does not yet show reliable doorway/narrowing intelligence; a kitty may walk straight into a wall and stop. Treat this as a separate navigation risk when testing bed approach points so surface behavior is not blamed for a global pathing failure.

1
Does the bed need sprite cuts for the first pilot?

The newer hypothesis is that no occupied bed slot is visually behind or under a separate bed part. If that is true, splitting the art is unnecessary until the no-cut pilot fails.

2
How should a kitty get onto the bed?

Normal navigation understands floors and obstacles. A bed surface is not just another walk target; it is a mounted interaction.

3
How should floor and mounted kitties render?

Once a kitty is on the bed, ordinary WorldLayer y-sort should no longer be the only draw-order rule. The bed can use different render treatment for floor traversal versus mounted occupancy.

4
When should this become generic furniture architecture?

The bed is the first raised surface. Sofas, chairs, windowsills, counters, and tubs may need similar behavior, but generalizing too early could bake in the wrong abstraction.

5
What collision model should the bed use?

The bed collision being on a different layer may be an accidental win: floor kitties can run under it, while mounted kitties are governed by hotspot occupancy instead of floor collision. This is a bed-specific decision, not the default for all furniture.

Furniture collision taxonomy to prove later: bed-like objects may allow pass-under floor movement, couches and cabinets should block, and tables may expose two different interaction modes: under-table floor hotspots and naughty on-table surface hotspots. If testing layer-1 leg collision, remember that the hotspot line-of-sight raycast currently scans layer 1 for walls, so tiny leg shapes must not accidentally hide valid hotspots.

Testing

  • With debug hotspots visible, left-click each SIT and LAY bed hotspot; the focused kitty selects the exact clicked slot.
  • Kitty first routes to a floor approach point, then visibly mounts onto the mattress/surface point.
  • After mounting, kitties do not always settle facing north; they sit first, use seated look-around/turn behavior, and LAY arrivals only lay down after that seated sequence.
  • When leaving from a mounted lay, the kitty sits up, uses seated look-around/turn behavior to face the dismount point, stands, and then dismounts.
  • Debug hotspots show both the occupied mattress point and the approach-to-occupy line for each surface slot.
  • Approach routing is tested both nearby and from across the room through doorways or narrowings; if the kitty walks into a wall and stops, log that as a navigation bug separate from the bed surface pilot.
  • While on the floor, a kitty can pass under/behind the bed sprite without looking like it is on top of the mattress.
  • While mounted, the kitty draws above the whole bed sprite and reads as sitting or lying on the mattress.
  • Optional tiny bed-leg collision does not break hotspot line-of-sight checks or cause the kitty to snag while passing under the bed.
  • No occupied bed slot appears hidden behind or under a bed part. If one does, Q1 returns to the split-sprite path.
  • Leaving the bed visibly dismounts to the floor point, releases the claimed slot, and returns the kitty to normal floor behavior before pathing onward.
  • Another kitty cannot claim an occupied bed slot.
  • Normal kitty-vs-kitty y-sort still works away from furniture.
  • Godot output log has no new script errors after five minutes of autonomous play with the bed in the room.
